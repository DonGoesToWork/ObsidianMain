Energy System:

The game uses an 'energy' system where every action that you perform takes a certain amount of energy to complete. After you run out of energy, the next day starts. And, depending on very many factors, the energy cost of actions can go up and down and your total energy can even be modified.

Multiplayer:

The game was designed to be multiplayer from the start with even single-player mode technically starting up a local client and local server that are connected to each other (the Minecraft design if you will). As such, the game supports multiplayer with an infinite number of other people out of the box and single-player worlds can be converted to multiplayer and back at any time. Single-player and multiplayer characters are saved separately from each other, as multiplayer characters are uniquely not bound to a specific world and players will always have access to their multiplayer characters, regardless of the server they are or are not connected to.

My vision with MP is that a small group of friends can join the game and roleplay as any type of persona they want. Thus, players are coded as independent entities in multiplayer that can act quite freely. There is a party system that can be used to group up with others to stick with them more easily (the party leader dictates everything), however, parties are entirely optional and don't necessarily prevent you from engaging in pvp, hitting with friendly fire, joining/leaving encounters (if there are 4+ other players, you can flee at any time instantly using them as a distraction (unless physically incapable ofc)), using actions, etc. It's entirely possible to play as a co-op band of friends adventuring together, or as independent adventurers with the sole goal of destroying one another (Activity reporting is optional).

The game host has the ability to kick players from a server at will. And, if game hosts leaves, a new host is picked from whomever else is online.
