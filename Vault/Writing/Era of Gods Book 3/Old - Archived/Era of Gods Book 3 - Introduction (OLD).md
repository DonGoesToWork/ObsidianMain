# End of Gods
Volume 3: A Balanced Restart

# Introduction
Era of Gods is a horror fantasy set in a virtual game world known as Era of Gods. It tells the story of glory and conquest that its inhabitants experience as they do their best to survive in a world determined to kill them. Will they live? Or, will they die? Only time will tell.
This second book continues from the last and leads up to on the journey of Mary, a true hyper-scaler that exists to win the game.
This book begins with a lot more technical detail than the last, because it’s simply necessary to explain everything in detail to fully flesh out the perk systems. But, after the first chapter, things will chill out once again.

Even though there were many changes to the balancing of perks, the perk selection system’s mechanics are roughly the same as before. Curses grant additional perks, perk scaling curves can be customized, dormant power-like perks can still be picked and randomization can produce extremely strong results.
There were the many nerfs that Al mentioned earlier. But, there are also some buffs, like how many perks can be quite strong early on without sacrificing too much late game scaling potential anymore. Mary doesn’t necessarily like this flattened power scheme… but it is fairer and more balanced, so she can only accept it and work around the changes.
All of the changes have resulted in Mary choosing a solid, strong build that takes advantage of various systems to create a strong early, mid and late game build. Normal players are less likely to win than exceptional players, which is why she didn’t settle for being normal last time and won’t settle for normalcy this time either.
And besides… playing and losing normally wouldn’t be fun. Thus, to get started with, she began by picking curses.
Curses are generally the easiest and most straightforward way to get additional perk power. And, with players being limited to 3 perks at the start, curses are more potent than ever… though, she definitely is suspicious about how soon she will get additional perks from leveling given the fact that curses seems way too good as is.
She picked the Lawful Good, Warrior Specialization and Prey Curses because they have strong drawbacks, but generally do not interfere with her desired playstyle.
Being Lawful Good generally results in positive outcomes. Warrior Specialization Curse is very restrictive, but is fine if you intend to be a warrior. And last, the Prey Curse can suck, but mostly only if you care about remaining hidden or concealing your strength. Since Mary intends to take on the ‘Lawful God Fighter’ persona, the only problem would be if it enables strong enemies to find and target her more easily. But, if she winds up joining a large group, which she fully intends to do, then even this curse will eventually be mitigated by stronger group effects.
With those three curses granting a net benefit of 3 perk points, she then went on to spend her remaiing perk points.
Super Form is her obvious first pick choice because it’s pretty much mandatory for any serious competitive build. Even a build with 3 Super Form-like perks can be considered extremely strong, which really goes to show how valuable such a perk really is. But, Mary doesn’t want ‘extremely strong’; she wants borderline broken. Thus, she went ahead and grabbed 3 other perks instead.
War Mode is her preperation perk that will enable her to more consistently survive and clear hard combat encounters. Plus, because it is team-oriented, her team will become more capable and she will even gain social benefits from it as well when it comes to joining groups.
Heavy Fighter Class Specialization is a more specialized version of the Fighter Class Specialization perk and is an option to achieve maximum synergy, benefit and ability with Mary’s literal only available playstyle: melee fighter.
No Weak Spots is a popular defensive perk that provides the greatest overall defensive benefit against the largest variety of killing threats. It won’t protect against extreme instances of falling, crushing, poison, impaling, elemental or other damage. But, it can provide immunity against weak sources, protection from injury against mild sources and potentially negate death in moderate cases. Overall, it’s a great perk for a frontlinng melee-oriented character to have to prevent a quick death. And, since Mary isn't picking any escape perks, this kind of perk can improve her lifespan dramatically.
Finally, to bring all of these perks together, she grabbed Onslaught. This perk makes it much easier to fight indefinitely and at higher speeds while also providing an outlet for excess physical energy to be utilized at higher intensity. However, even outside of combat, its stamina and health regeneration effects shouldn't be ignored. The stamina regeneraiton provides tremendous convenience  while traveling. And, the health regeneration can restore missing limbs, heal poisons and achieve a lot of great feats. Furthermore, the slow heal rate can appear unappealing, but it has to be remembered that it is a rate for healing one's ENTIRE body per second. If the healing effect is operating only on a single finger, then the real healing rate will be quite high. Consequently, if a person is affected by a disease that spreads faster than it can be healed or affected by a constant effect, like being burned alive, then the healing effect could be anywhere from poor to virtually useless.
Even still, she doesn't doubt that there will be plenty of Healers this time around due to their popularity in the last game session, so she almost doesn't want to get this perk. However, if she were to assume that there will be a team member with a class to address every one of her needs, then which perks would be left for her to pick? Only team buffing perks, really. But, she knows that such a build isn't her ideal. She wants to be a Juggernaut; capable of running into the fray and cleaving apart every enemy in sight. The support that she recieves from her team shoudl be an accessory, not a necessity.
After those fairly standard perks, she has Sealed Power II, which is her late-game scaling and gamble to win. By delaying the effect to level 50 and partially randomizing the result, she expects to gain about 50% more power from the perk than normal. Thus, at level 50, she is hoping to gain about 4 perks worth of power, bringing her overall power level up to 12 perks worth of power.
And, if she gains any more perks from levels like in the last game session, then she'll reach even greater heights.
“Yeah, these have to be the ones,” Mary states as she picks her appearance, sets her race, allocates her base stats and confirms everything.
She has spent over a week playtesting various perks, the new game balance and mechanics and feels that these choices are ideal. Thus, she has no reservations about her choices.
